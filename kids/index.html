01 — Speed

How fast are these cars?!

Buckle up. These numbers are going to blow your mind.

⚡
230+ mph
Top Speed

That's faster than a cheetah (the fastest animal on land), faster than most planes when they take off, and way faster than your parents drive on the highway.

💨
1.80s
Pit Stop Record

McLaren changed all 4 tires in 1.80 seconds. Try counting to two — they'd already be done. Twenty people working at once, each with one job.

🎯
6G
G-Force on Drivers

That's 6 times the pull of gravity! Imagine 6 copies of yourself sitting on your chest. Fighter pilots wear special suits for this. F1 drivers don't.

🌏
827M
Fans Worldwide

827 million people watch F1 around the world. That's more than the entire population of Europe! Races happen in 24 countries across 5 continents.

"F1 cars make so much downforce they could literally drive upside-down on the ceiling of a tunnel."

Here's the wildest part: F1 cars push themselves INTO the ground so hard they could drive upside-down on a ceiling. It's called downforce — the car's wings work like an upside-down airplane wing, pushing down instead of lifting up.

Drivers go from 200 mph to 50 mph in about 2 seconds, while their hearts beat as fast as yours does when you sprint flat out. And they do this for nearly two hours straight!

How fast can an F1 car go?

Could an F1 car really drive upside-down?
Yes! The downforce (the invisible push from the car's wings) is stronger than the car's own weight, so in theory it could stick to a ceiling.
How many people watch F1?
About 827 million people worldwide — that's more than the population of Europe!

04 — The Cars

Incredible machines

Every team builds a brand new car each year. No two are exactly alike.

Here's something cool: the car matters MORE than the driver in F1. A great driver in a slow car will struggle. That's why the engineering battle between teams is just as exciting as the racing itself.

Each car has over 14,500 individual parts and the steering wheel alone costs about $100,000 — it has more than 25 buttons and looks more like a video game controller than a regular steering wheel!

The rear wing with moving parts!
Precision engineering at speed

Wings that actually MOVE!

Starting in 2026, F1 cars have active aerodynamics — that means parts of the car's wings can change shape while the car is driving! Think of it like a bird adjusting its wings.

💪
Z-Mode
Maximum Grip
The wings open up wide to push the car into the ground. This helps drivers go around corners at incredible speeds without flying off the track.
🚀
X-Mode
Maximum Speed
The wings flatten out to slice through the air. Less grip, but way more speed on the straights. Perfect for overtaking!

The Halo — a life saver

See that curved bar above the driver's head? That's the Halo. It's made from titanium (the same stuff in spacecraft!) and can hold the weight of a double-decker bus. It's saved multiple drivers' lives since it was introduced in 2018.

Did you know? F1 cars are actually partly electric! They use a hybrid engine that captures energy from braking and the exhaust. In 2026, the electric motor will be even more powerful — the cars will be half electric and half fuel-powered.

🧠 Test Your Knowledge!
How many parts does an F1 car have?
Over 14,500 individual parts! Each one is carefully designed and handmade.
What does the Halo do?
The Halo is a titanium safety device above the driver's head. It protects them from flying objects and has saved several lives.
What's the difference between Z-Mode and X-Mode?
Z-Mode gives maximum downforce (grip) for corners. X-Mode reduces drag for maximum speed on straights. The wings physically change shape!

06 — Race Day

How a race works

Three days of action. Here's how it all goes down.

Friday
📋
Practice

Teams test different setups and the drivers learn the track. It's like homework before the big test!

Saturday
⏱
Qualifying

Drivers try to set the fastest single lap. The fastest driver starts at the front on Sunday — that's called "pole position."

Sunday
🏁
Race Day!

Five red lights go out and it's GO! About 50–70 laps of racing, pit stops, overtakes, and drama. This is what it's all about.

Points: how you become champion

The top 10 finishers in each race earn points. At the end of the season (24 races!), whoever has the most points wins the World Championship.

5 coolest tracks in the world

F1 races happen all around the world — 24 different countries in 2026! Here are some of the most famous ones:

Monaco
Racing through the streets of Monte Carlo! So narrow that overtaking is nearly impossible. The most glamorous race on the calendar.
Silverstone (UK)
The home of F1! This is where the very first Grand Prix was held in 1950. Super-fast corners that test drivers' bravery.
Spa (Belgium)
Famous for "Eau Rouge" — a scary uphill corner taken at full speed. The weather can be sunny on one side of the track and raining on the other!
Monza (Italy)
Called the "Temple of Speed" because it's one of the fastest tracks ever. Ferrari's home race — the atmosphere is absolutely electric!
Suzuka (Japan)
The only figure-eight shaped track in F1! The road actually crosses over itself. Japanese fans are some of the most dedicated in the world.
🧠 Test Your Knowledge!
How many points does the winner of a race get?
25 points! Second place gets 18 and third place gets 15.
What is "pole position"?
Starting first on the grid! The driver who sets the fastest lap in qualifying earns pole position for the race.
Which track is shaped like a figure eight?
Suzuka in Japan! The track crosses over itself, making it unique in all of motorsport.

Famous radio moments

One of the coolest things about F1 is team radio — you can actually hear drivers talking to their teams during the race! They shout, celebrate, complain, and sometimes say really funny things at 200 mph. No other sport lets you hear what athletes are thinking in real time.

Classic moment: When a driver named Kimi Räikkönen was told over the radio what his teammate was doing, he famously replied: "Leave me alone, I know what I'm doing." He won the race!
